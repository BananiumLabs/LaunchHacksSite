About

Launch Hacks is a 24 hour, 200-participant hackathon hosted by Monta Vista High School students. Our goal is to bring high school students in the Bay Area together to convert ideas into reality. In addition to helping students improve their design and development skills, we also provide support to help students improve business and presentation skills- not just to pitch a successful project, but also to inspire the next generation of inventors, entrepreneurs, and leaders. Food, workshops, prizes, and swag will be provided to all participants for free!

FAQ

Who can attend?

LaunchHacks is open to all high school students (9th to 12th grade). No experience is necessary, and we would love to see new participants! However, if you have already graduated from high school, you can still attend as a mentor!

Do I have to pay for anything?

Attending LaunchHacks is and will always be free! Leave your money at home, as we won't be selling you anything :)

What can I get from coming?

In addition to a fun and unique experience to improve your maker skills with your friends, we will have amazing prizes for our best submissions! And even if you don't end up winning, everyone will get free food and swag, including T-shirts, laptop stickers, and various goodies from our sponsors!

What should I bring?

All you need to bring is a laptop to work on, a student ID from your school, a sleeping bag or blanket, toiletries, and any accessories you might want (headphones, chargers, cool hardware to work with, etc). We'll provide the food, swag, prizes, and mentors for free!

What can I make?

Anything you want! Software projects are often the most popular type of submission, but we also welcome and encourage other types of unique submissions, like hardware (Arduinos, Raspberry Pi, etc), invention prototypes, artwork, or anything else you can think of. Even if you can't or don't want to code, there are still plenty of things you can make to wow the judges!

How many people can be in a team?

Teams can be a maximum of 4 people. Solo teams are allowed, but we strongly encourage you to meet new people if you come by yourself! On the other hand, if you are a team of 2 or 3, one of the best things you can do is invite someone new to join your team - they will bring in new ideas and areas of expertise. At the beginning of the event, we will do our best to help everyone find a team.

Am I required to stay overnight?

If you need to leave early for any reason, please let us know through email before the day of the event. For safety reasons we will not allow anyone to leave after 9 PM. Doors will re-open at 9 AM in time for judging and final submissions.

Do I have to know how to code?

Definitely not! If you don't feel like coding, there are still so many things you can make - see 'What can I make?' for a couple ideas. If you do want to code something but don't know how yet, don't worry! We will hold workshops and provide expert mentors to help you get started.

How will our project be judged?

All projects will be judged with the same criteria: originality, polish, difficulty of implementation, and viability as a product. We highly suggest that you treat your project like a product that you could potentially sell- rather than pitching just for the judges, pitch for everyone else, too!

I have more questions, help me!

Please feel free to join the official LaunchHacks Discord channel at: launchhacks.tech/discord or feel free to email us at contact@launchhacks.tech!

Why all the doggos?

Doggos are much cuter than empty space, wouldn't you agree? Don't worry- as we get closer to the event date, our doggos will slowly be replaced with the people, companies, and organizations that we would like to honor for their amazing support to turn our dream of hosting a hackathon into reality.
